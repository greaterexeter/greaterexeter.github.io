About

Think local, act global

We face complex, interdependent social, economic and environmental challenges that do not recognise local, national or international authority remits or jurisdictions.

They reach far beyond the scope of individual corporate strategies and elude NGOs and charities, let alone community groups and individual citizens.

Neither electorates nor markets have successfully channelled the forces of globalisation, allowing nativist sentimentality and personal identity politics to fill the vacuum.

While Exeter has developed significant strengths during the past decade, the future demands renewed strategic vision and the courage to pursue imaginative ideas with transformative potential.

A place-based perspective offers to disaggregate the complexity, while supporting direct focus on locally-specific tasks. The scale at which people live, work, form families and start companies offers the best basis on which to enable change.

Instead of relying on governance alone to solve our problems, individuals, businesses and organisations of all shapes and sizes can collaborate around common interest.

Understanding our potential in this way helps leverage public, private and community resources so we become greater together than the sum of our individual parts.

Who is better placed to make the most of meeting the social, economic and environmental needs of the emerging Greater Exeter than the people who live and work here?

Strategies and solutions for inclusive growth

Greater Exeter CIC is an independent not-for-profit social enterprise dedicated to creating sustainable productive prosperity for all who live and work in and around Exeter.

It seeks to develop strategies and solutions for inclusive growth that combine social, economic and environmental factors to create positive outcomes that include all sections of the community and ensure no-one gets left behind in a rapidly-changing environment.

It is the brainchild of a group of experienced consultants with local expertise in economy, transport, planning, public policy, education, health, business development and communications.

It seeks to faciliate collaboration between private, public, third sector decision-makers, business networks, community groups, advocacy, campaigning and non-profit organisations, NGOs, charities, social enterprises, residents, workers and visitors.

Participatory action research

Insight

Peering through the wrong end of the telescope leads to lazy and misleading marketing and public relations puff.

Instead we need significant differentiating insights based on relevant, rigorous, realistic appraisal and comprehensive situational analysis which challenges assumptions and delivers a reality/clarity check against likely future scenarios.

True strategic intelligence transcends silos and straddles spheres to offer real insight into the complex, changing multi-stakeholder environment in which we operate.

Vision

On its own, no amount of insight will do anything other than reveal the status quo.

We also need the imagination and courage to create renewed strategic vision and discover interventions with real potential for positive impact.

Agile, experimental, peer to peer, open source, whole system change facilitation is needed to assemble and communicate compelling change narratives across the stakeholder landscape via comprehensive, impartial insight and information.

Impact

Discovering effective impact interventions to implement renewed vision involves identifying novel approaches that provide demonstrable, measurable benefits elsewhere and can be successfully transplanted to meet specific local challenges.

Adopting participative methodologies which test and measure repeatable, real-world outcomes steers clear of blue pie in the sky thinking or reinventing the wheel.

Promoting new ideas, thinking and vision and facilitating multi-stakeholder common interest implementation supports inclusive future-oriented thought leadership.
